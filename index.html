About Me

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Statistics at Harvard University I am advised by Professor Iavor Bojinov and Lucas Janson I am broadly interested in statistical inference for adaptive experiments, causal inference, and online reinforcement learning. Prior to Harvard, I worked as a Data Scientist at Instagram. Before that, I received my Bachelor's from UC Berkeley, where I was awarded the Departmental Citation in Statistics. During my Ph.D., I was a biostatistics intern at Moderna working on time-to-event prediction and a statistical consultant for MDRC working on heterogenous treatment effect detection. I am a founding member of Data Adventure Day at Harvard, an event is designed to expose high school students to statistics and data science through hands-on activities and interactions with Harvard students, faculty, and staff.
