About Me

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Statistics at Harvard University advised by Professor Iavor Bojinov and Lucas Janson. I am broadly interested in reinforcement learning, causal inference, and statistical inference for adaptive experimentation. Prior to Harvard, I was a Data Scientist at Meta. Before that, I received my Bachelor's from UC Berkeley, where I was awarded the 2019 Departmental Citation in Statistics, the highest honor awarded to a single graduating senior from each department at UC Berkeley. During my Ph.D., I was a biostatistics intern at Moderna working on clinical trial planning and a statistical consultant at MDRC working on heterogenous treatment effect detection.
